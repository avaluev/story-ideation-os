# Anomaly 1 — Boeing John Barnett Whistleblower Death

**Tier-1 PRIMARY sources:**
- Charleston County Coroner — confirmed cause of death March 2024 (suicide), with toxicology and time-of-death records
- OSHA AIR21 retaliation case file — Barnett's complaint against Boeing, deposition transcripts (his testimony was completed 24h before death)
- NTSB & FAA Boeing 787/737 quality investigations — Barnett's specific allegations about defective fasteners, oxygen masks, and traceability
- AP / Reuters / NYT 2024 reporting — the documented sequence of events

**300-word summary:**

John "Mitch" Barnett was a Boeing quality-control manager for 32 years before retiring in 2017, after which he filed a whistleblower retaliation claim under AIR21 (the Aviation Investment and Reform Act for the 21st Century). His complaint alleged Boeing's North Charleston 787 plant violated quality controls — defective fasteners installed by overworked teams, oxygen-mask systems with 25% failure rates suppressed from internal review, missing parts deliberately uncategorized to ship aircraft on schedule.

In March 2024, Barnett gave depositions to Boeing's defense attorneys for two consecutive days at a Holiday Inn in North Charleston. On day three he was found dead in the hotel parking lot in his pickup truck. The Charleston County Coroner ruled it a self-inflicted gunshot wound; family attorneys publicly stated Barnett had told them: *"if anything happens to me, it's not suicide."* The deposition transcripts — his testimony 24 hours before death — became part of the discovery record in his ongoing AIR21 claim.

Three months later, in May 2024, a SECOND Boeing whistleblower (Joshua Dean, 45, former Spirit AeroSystems quality auditor in Wichita) died of a sudden bacterial infection (MRSA-staph) days after testifying about Boeing 737 MAX rudder-control defects.
