# Anomaly 2 — Norfolk Southern East Palestine Ohio Derailment

**Tier-1 PRIMARY sources:**
- NTSB Final Report on East Palestine Derailment — released June 2024; identifies wheel-bearing failure + cascading hot-box detector spacing + vinyl-chloride controlled-burn decision
- EPA Federal Register orders to Norfolk Southern — soil and groundwater remediation timelines + chemical inventory
- Senate Commerce Committee Hearings (March 2023, May 2024) — sworn testimony from Norfolk Southern CEO Alan Shaw and EPA Region 5
- DOJ + EPA settlement (May 2024, $310M) — the consent decree

**300-word summary:**

On February 3, 2023, Norfolk Southern train 32N — 149 cars carrying hazardous chemicals including vinyl chloride, butyl acrylate, and 2-ethylhexyl acrylate — derailed in East Palestine, Ohio, a town of 4,700 people on the Pennsylvania border. NTSB later identified a wheel-bearing failure on car 23, and noted that hot-box detectors (which monitor bearing temperature) were spaced ~30 miles apart; the bearing had been overheating across the prior detector readings but had not yet hit Norfolk Southern's internal alarm threshold.

The decision to perform a "controlled vent and burn" of vinyl chloride from five tank cars on February 6 — to prevent BLEVE explosion — released a black plume visible 30 miles away and deposited dioxin and phosgene combustion products across a multi-county area. Norfolk Southern's incident commander Drew McCarty made the burn-decision under federal Hazardous Materials authority but with no community evacuation beyond a 1-mile radius.

In the year following: residents reported chemical headaches, livestock death, fish kills in tributaries to the Ohio River; CDC published preliminary biomonitoring data; the EPA mandated Norfolk Southern fund water/soil/air monitoring through 2050. The May 2024 DOJ/EPA settlement of $310M was widely criticized as insufficient. NTSB issued 11 safety recommendations including mandatory hot-box detector spacing reform.
