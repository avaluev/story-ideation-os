# Anomaly 4 — OpenAI November 2023 Board Coup

**Tier-1 PRIMARY sources:**
- OpenAI 501(c)(3) board statement (November 17, 2023) and subsequent corrections
- Microsoft 13D filing language and corporate disclosures (Q4 2023 / Q1 2024)
- Employee letter (November 20, 2023) — 743 of 770 employees signed (>96%)
- WilmerHale independent investigation summary (released March 2024)
- NYT/The Information/Bloomberg detailed reconstructions (Nov 2023 – Mar 2024)

**300-word summary:**

On Friday November 17, 2023 at approximately 11:47am Pacific, OpenAI's nonprofit board (Adam D'Angelo, Tasha McCauley, Helen Toner, Ilya Sutskever) fired co-founder and CEO Sam Altman via Google Meet. The public board statement said Altman "was not consistently candid in his communications with the board." Greg Brockman (board chair, co-founder) was simultaneously removed from the board; he resigned hours later.

Over the next 96 hours: Microsoft (then-largest investor with $13B committed) announced Altman and Brockman would lead a new advanced AI research lab inside Microsoft. 743 of 770 OpenAI employees signed an open letter demanding the board resign and Altman return; signatories included Ilya Sutskever himself, who later said he regretted his role in the firing. By Wednesday November 22, the board had reconstituted (Bret Taylor chair, Larry Summers, Adam D'Angelo retained) and Altman was reinstated.

The WilmerHale March 2024 review concluded the board's decision was a "consequence of a breakdown in trust" but identified no fraudulent or unsafe conduct by Altman. Helen Toner (Georgetown CSET researcher who had co-authored a paper criticizing OpenAI before the firing) became a public face of the AI-safety position; in subsequent interviews she described the board's concerns about Altman's "psychological abuse" of senior executives — a claim Altman has denied.

Ilya Sutskever left OpenAI in May 2024 to found Safe Superintelligence Inc., a $1B-funded AI-safety lab.
