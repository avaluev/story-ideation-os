# Investor Brief Template — 11-Section Structure

> Industry-standard film investor document structure. Maps to the v5
> rendering targets at Stage D: `1PAGE_BRIEF.md` (compressed) and
> `AUDIENCE_APPENDIX.md` (expanded). Sourced from agent_4 (market signals)
> + investor-pitch-deck industry conventions (Sequoia film template, Slated
> investor-doc spec, the "Reverse-engineering top 3000 films" research that
> the prior 7-agent pipeline distilled).

---

## §1 — Logline (≤60 words, ends on concrete binary per L010)

The 1-2 sentence pitch. Protagonist + irreversible obstacle + stakes + concrete-binary close. The single most important sentence in the document — investors read the logline and decide whether to read the next paragraph.

**Test:** Can the listener, after hearing 25 words, ask "what happens next?" If they ask "what does that mean?", the logline failed (PM-D11/D12 violation).

**Boeing "The Fastener" example:** "A retired Boeing quality manager spends the last seventy-two hours of his life in a Holiday Inn deposition room, knowing that the daughter he never told — a Boeing engineer signing off on the same defective 787 fuselage line — will inherit either his testimony or his silence."

---

## §2 — Synopsis (1 page, three-act with mirrored final image)

3-4 paragraphs. Acts I, II, III plus the indelible image. Per L009: ≥5 specific sensory elements in the indelible image.

Structure:
- **Act I** — opening image specified; inciting event; protagonist crosses threshold
- **Act II** — midpoint reversal; antagonist moves
- **Act III** — climax (DECISION under pressure per L016); final image mirrors opening with transformation visible

---

## §3 — Comparables (3 successful + 1 cautionary)

Per agent_6 §6.5 (survivorship-bias audit). Always include cautionary comp to avoid pure-success-pattern training-data attractor.

Format per comp:
- **Title (year, distributor, US opening, US total domestic, audience CinemaScore, critic RT%)**
- 1-line: what this film proves about audience appetite for the v5 concept

**3 successful** — recent (last 24 months ideal) + same vehicle + same audience scope
**1 cautionary** — a structurally similar film that FAILED commercially; what it teaches the v5 concept to avoid

Boeing example:
- *Erin Brockovich* (2000, Universal, $28M open, $125M domestic, A CinemaScore, 84% RT) — mass-tort drama with named individual antagonist
- *The Insider* (1999, Touchstone, $6.7M open, $29M domestic, B+ CinemaScore, 96% RT) — corporate-whistleblower drama
- *Spotlight* (2015, Open Road, $4.5M open, $45M domestic, A- CinemaScore, 96% RT) — institutional-failure procedural
- **Cautionary:** *Concussion* (2015, Sony, $11M open, $34M domestic, A- CinemaScore, 60% RT) — corporate-truth drama that underperformed because Will Smith's star casting created audience expectation mismatch

## §4 — Audience Analysis (segments + sizing, Tier-1 sources)

Per L001 + R004 + R202. Maps to `AUDIENCE_APPENDIX.md`. Three named US/UK/CA/AU/NZ/IE segments minimum, summing to ≥30M addressable adults, every number cited.

Structure (per segment):
- **Demographics** — age, geo, occupation, education (with source URL)
- **Why this segment shows up for THIS film** — 2-3 paragraphs, each citing a specific EVIDENCE.md fact
- **Tonal contract** they expect (per agent_6 PM-D10)
- **What kills it for this segment** — 1-2 deal-breakers from PM-D list

NEVER cite aggregate populations (no "1.8B Muslims globally"). NEVER cite world-religion totals. Always per-country breakdown.

---

## §5 — Market Timing Thesis (zeitgeist alignment)

Per agent_4 §4.3. Operationalize "is the cultural moment NOW for this concept?"

Required components:
- **Current discourse** — 1 paragraph citing recent news / trade-press / social-media data, ALL with URLs
- **Adjacent films released in last 24 months** — 3 with US opening + total domestic + critic/audience scores
- **Forecast** — is the discourse rising or falling? cite a measurable indicator (Google Trends, FRED, trade-press coverage volume)
- **Production-window alignment** — if the film takes 2-3 years to produce, will the discourse still be live at release?

Examples of strong zeitgeist alignment:
- *Squid Game* (2021): writer held concept 10 years for the right moment; COVID-era debt anxiety made it land
- *Joker* (2019): incel-era discourse provided "free media" context
- *Barbie* (2023): post-Roe feminist discourse
- *Parasite* (2019): peak inequality-discourse year (Davos 2019-2020)

---

## §6 — Talent Attached / Wishlist

Per agent_4 §4.5. Format:
- **Director (1):** attached or wishlist (2 alternates)
- **Lead (1):** attached or wishlist (3 alternates) + casting rationale anchored to character
- **Supporting (2-3):** wishlist
- **Producer (1):** typically the operator + 1 industry attachment

CRITICAL per agent_4: star power is mostly selection bias. Concept quality dominates the long run. Do NOT over-leverage star casting; choose stars whose track record matches the film's tonal contract.

Boeing example:
- **Director wishlist:** Tony Gilroy (*Michael Clayton*) → Sean Durkin (*Iron Claw*) → Steven Soderbergh (*The Insider* / *Erin Brockovich* tonal lineage)
- **Lead (Mitch):** Bryan Cranston, John C. Reilly, Brendan Gleeson
- **Antagonist (Karen):** Viola Davis, Marisa Tomei, Jodie Foster

---

## §7 — Production Budget Bands (3 scenarios)

Per L005 (vehicle-fork). Each scenario has the same logline but different production scale:

- **Theatrical-auteur** ($5-15M, A24/Plan B/Searchlight) — 95% one-location, restrained scope, 30-40 day shoot
- **Theatrical-broad** ($30-80M, Imagine/Working Title/MGM) — multi-location, supporting cast expansion, 60-80 day shoot
- **Limited-series** ($4-15M/ep, FX/Hulu/Apple/HBO) — 6-8 episodes, period detail, supporting cast expansion

Each band has a different ROI structure and a different distributor target. Operator picks ONE based on which financier conversation is live.

---

## §8 — Distribution Strategy

3 paragraphs:
- **Theatrical positioning** — opening weekend target (number of screens, target audiences for first 3 days)
- **Streaming window** — typical 45-90 day theatrical → streaming (varies by distributor)
- **International rollout** — culturally-discount-adjusted territory plan (per agent_4 §4.6)

For limited-series: pivot from "opening weekend" to "premiere week + linear/digital simultaneous" pattern.

---

## §9 — Risk Register (5-7 named risks with mitigations)

Per agent_6 PM-D rules. Each risk:
- **Risk** (1 sentence)
- **Severity** (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW)
- **Mitigation pathway** (concrete action)
- **Trigger to revisit** (what observable would force the risk back to active)

Standard risks:
1. Concept-execution gap (PM-D03/04) — mitigation: greenlit director with track record matching tonal contract
2. Genre confusion (PM-D06) — mitigation: tightened logline + matching trailer cuts
3. Living-figure legal exposure (L004) — mitigation: composite-rename + clearance review
4. Tonal mismatch in marketing (PM-D10) — mitigation: marketing partner with proven category track record
5. Cultural backlash (PM-D14/15) — mitigation: publicity strategy that channels rather than suppresses controversy

---

## §10 — Financing Structure (high-level)

For independent / low-mid-budget concepts:
- **Equity %** — typical 30-50% for indie / 50-70% for studio film
- **Tax credit %** — typical 10-30% (varies by jurisdiction; Georgia / NYC / UK / Canada / Hungary common)
- **Pre-sale %** — international territory pre-sales typical 10-30%
- **Gap finance %** — last-mile typical 5-15%

NOTE: this section is the operator's domain. The engine should NOT generate fictitious financing structures; this section gets filled in manually OR by the operator's financial-modeler agent (separate from the v5 ideation engine).

---

## §11 — Footnotes / Sources

Every numeric claim in §1-§10 must trace to either:
- A specific EVIDENCE.md row ID (F1.1, F2.3, etc.) for evidence-anchored claims
- A Tier-1 URL (Pew / Census / ONS / StatsCan / SEC / court) for audience or financial data
- A specific The-Numbers / Box Office Mojo URL for comparable BO data

Format: `[N] {claim} ({source URL or EVIDENCE row})`. Audit by `evals/test_evidence_anchored.py`.

## How v5 Stage D Haiku rendering uses this

When operator marks FINAL.md, Stage D Haiku reads:
- FINAL.md (the chosen iter)
- EVIDENCE.md (the cluster)
- FORMULA.md (the structural template)

And renders:
- `30SEC_PITCH.md` — §1 logline + §6 director/lead, ~80 spoken words
- `1PAGE_BRIEF.md` — §1 + §2 + §3 + §4 (compressed) + §6 + §9 (compressed); 8 sections per HANDOFF.md template
- `AUDIENCE_APPENDIX.md` — §4 (full) + §5 (full); 7 sections per ULTRAPLAN_v5.md §7

The 11-section template here is the SUPERSET. The 1PAGE_BRIEF is the operator-pitch artifact (compressed); the AUDIENCE_APPENDIX is the financier-pitch artifact (expanded). The full 11-section investor brief is generated only when the operator escalates to a real financier conversation, not on every FINAL.
