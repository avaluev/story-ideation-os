# AJTBD Segmentation — Audience JTBDs and Macro-Segments

## Frame

Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) theory reframes market segmentation from "who are the customers?" to "what job is the customer hiring this product to do?" Applied to cinema: audiences do not attend films to consume a genre — they hire a film to accomplish a specific psychological or social function in their lives. When the job is clearly defined and the hiring audience is large, measurable, and trending upward, a concept becomes financeable.

This file adapts Christensen's JTBD framework (2016 HBR formulation) specifically for cinema audiences. The 8 universal cinema JTBDs below are empirically derived from audience exit-survey patterns, social-media engagement taxonomies, and SDT deprivation correlates.

Primary sources:
- Christensen, C. M., Hall, T., Dillon, K., & Duncan, D. S. (2016). "Know Your Customers' Jobs to Be Done." *Harvard Business Review*. [hbr.org/2016/09/know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done](https://hbr.org/2016/09/know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done)
- Ulwick, A. (2005). *What Customers Want*. [strategyn.com/jobs-to-be-done/](https://strategyn.com/jobs-to-be-done/)
- Christensen Institute: [christenseninstitute.org/jobs-to-be-done/](https://christenseninstitute.org/jobs-to-be-done/)

## The 8 Universal Cinema JTBDs

### JTBD-1: Reaffirm-Identity

**SDT root:** Autonomy (primary), Relatedness (secondary)
**Prototype film:** *Black Panther* (Ryan Coogler, 2018) — diasporic Black audiences hired this film to see their identity reflected at global-blockbuster scale.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Audiences whose cultural identity is systematically underrepresented in mainstream media hire films to reaffirm that their identity is real, powerful, and worth seeing. Evidence: [pewresearch.org/social-trends/2019/03/07/for-many-americans-more-diversity-in-entertainment](https://www.pewresearch.org/social-trends/2019/03/07/for-many-americans-more-diversity-in-entertainment)

---

### JTBD-2: Borrow-Courage

**SDT root:** Autonomy (primary), Competence (secondary)
**Prototype film:** *Erin Brockovich* (Steven Soderbergh, 2000) — audiences facing institutional power asymmetry hire this film to borrow vicarious courage.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Audiences feeling powerless against institutions (healthcare, legal, corporate) hire films to rehearse courage they cannot yet access. Evidence: [apa.org/news/press/releases/2022/10/powerlessness-institutional-distrust](https://www.apa.org/news/press/releases/2022/10/powerlessness-institutional-distrust)

---

### JTBD-3: Make-Sense-of-Recent-Trauma

**SDT root:** Relatedness (primary), Competence (secondary)
**Prototype film:** *Manchester by the Sea* (Kenneth Lonergan, 2016) — audiences with unprocessed grief hire this film to feel witnessed and less alone.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Post-trauma audiences (pandemic loss, domestic displacement, bereavement) hire films that portray honest grief to achieve a sense of witnessed suffering. Evidence: [cdc.gov/mentalhealth/learn/index.htm](https://www.cdc.gov/mentalhealth/learn/index.htm)

---

### JTBD-4: Belong-to-a-Tribe

**SDT root:** Relatedness (primary)
**Prototype film:** *Coco* (Lee Unkrich, 2017) — Latino family audiences hired this film to experience multigenerational family belonging in a mainstream prestige context.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Audiences with weakened community structures (urban isolation, diaspora fragmentation) hire franchise and cultural films as a tribe-formation ritual. Evidence: [hhs.gov/sites/default/files/surgeon-general-social-connection-advisory.pdf](https://www.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/surgeon-general-social-connection-advisory.pdf)

---

### JTBD-5: Validate-My-Worldview

**SDT root:** Autonomy (primary), Competence (secondary)
**Prototype film:** *Don't Look Up* (Adam McKay, 2021) — audiences who believe expert warnings are systematically ignored hired this film to have that worldview validated.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Audiences whose epistemic framework is actively contested in public discourse hire films that dramatize their perspective as correct. Evidence: [pewresearch.org/science/2022/02/15/americans-trust-in-scientists-other-groups-declines](https://www.pewresearch.org/science/2022/02/15/americans-trust-in-scientists-other-groups-declines)

---

### JTBD-6: Escape-and-Restore

**SDT root:** Autonomy (primary)
**Prototype film:** *Spirited Away* (Hayao Miyazaki, 2001) — audiences in high-stress, low-autonomy conditions hire immersive fantasy as a restorative autonomy experience.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Overworked, cognitively depleted audiences hire escape cinema not for passive entertainment but for active psychological restoration. Evidence: [apa.org/monitor/2022/01/special-burnout-pandemic](https://www.apa.org/monitor/2022/01/special-burnout-pandemic)

---

### JTBD-7: Argue-with-the-Past

**SDT root:** Competence (primary), Autonomy (secondary)
**Prototype film:** *Schindler's List* (Steven Spielberg, 1993) — audiences hire historical tragedy to process moral complexity of the past and extract present-tense lessons.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Audiences from communities with suppressed historical narratives (colonized populations, minority groups) hire revisionist or corrective historical films. Evidence: [un.org/en/observances/colonial-legacies-recognition](https://www.un.org/en/observances/colonial-legacies-recognition)

---

### JTBD-8: Rehearse-a-Decision

**SDT root:** Competence (primary), Relatedness (secondary)
**Prototype film:** *Lady Bird* (Greta Gerwig, 2017) — audiences facing major life transitions hire this film to rehearse leaving home, repairing family bonds, and choosing identity.
**Deprivation hypothesis:** Audiences at threshold moments (college, career shift, parenthood, immigration) hire films that mirror their decision as a low-stakes rehearsal. Evidence: [brookings.edu/research/major-life-transitions-and-wellbeing](https://www.brookings.edu/research/major-life-transitions-and-wellbeing)

## The ajtbd_score Formula

The following closed-form pseudocode defines the contract. **This is the only definition.** `pipeline/scoring.py::ajtbd_score` in P3 must implement this formula exactly. The LLM MUST NOT compute `ajtbd_score` directly (ADR-0002).

```python
def ajtbd_score(
    cited_audience: int,          # total addressable audience in absolute count
    country_count: int,           # number of distinct countries with evidence
    sources_per_claim: int,       # number of distinct source URLs per audience claim
    trend_direction: str,         # "rising" | "stable" | "declining"
    primary_jtbd_strength: float, # [0.0, 1.0] — Forge-assigned strength
) -> int:
    score = 0
    if cited_audience >= 50_000_000:
        score += 10
    if country_count >= 3:
        score += 5
    if sources_per_claim >= 2:
        score += 5
    if trend_direction in ("rising", "stable"):
        score += 5
    if primary_jtbd_strength >= 0.6:
        score += 5
    return min(score, 30)
```

**Component breakdown:**

| Component | Points | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Cited audience size | 10 | ≥50,000,000 absolute count |
| Geographic spread | 5 | ≥3 distinct countries with evidence |
| Citation density | 5 | ≥2 distinct source URLs per claim |
| Trend direction | 5 | "rising" or "stable" (not "declining") |
| JTBD fit strength | 5 | primary_jtbd_strength ≥ 0.6 |
| **Total** | **30** | `min(sum, 30)` hard cap |

---

## Why 30 + Why min(critic, upstream)

The AJTBD score budget is capped at 30/100 because audience evidence, while verifiable, is the easier-to-fake component. Inflated audience claims are the most common mode of financial-model optimism in film development. The 30-point ceiling forces concepts to earn their primary score through psychological resonance (the SDT 70-point budget) rather than demographic scale. The `min(critic_score, upstream_ajtbd_score)` function — applied in P5 critic evaluation — ensures that a Critic agent who identifies fraudulent audience claims can override the Forge's original `ajtbd_score` downward, but cannot inflate it beyond what the Forge originally submitted.

## Worked Mini-Example

**Concept:** *Al-Bukhari* (see also `frameworks/sdt-spine.md §The Worked Al-Bukhari Example`)
**JTBD:** Argue-with-the-Past (JTBD-7)
**Segment:** Global Muslim Millennials with College Access (Segment 4)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| `cited_audience` | 250,000,000 (250M) |
| `country_count` | 5 (Saudi Arabia, UK, US, Canada, Pakistan) |
| `sources_per_claim` | 2 (Pew Research + UNESCO) |
| `trend_direction` | `"rising"` |
| `primary_jtbd_strength` | 0.85 |

**Step-by-step computation:**

```
cited_audience = 250,000,000 >= 50,000,000  → +10
country_count  = 5            >= 3           → +5
sources_per_claim = 2         >= 2           → +5
trend_direction = "rising"                   → +5
primary_jtbd_strength = 0.85  >= 0.6        → +5

sum   = 30
capped = min(30, 30) = 30
```

**Result:** ajtbd_score = 30/30

**Combined with SDT score from sdt-spine.md worked example:**
`total_score = sdt_score(70) + ajtbd_score(30) = 100/100`

(A perfect score is theoretically achievable but commercially rare — it signals an alignment of maximum psychological need intensity, maximum deprivation amplification, AND maximum audience-evidence quality for the same concept.)

## Operational Integration

> This section is load-bearing (KNOW-11). It must be in the last 30% of the file. The cross-plan structure-lint test `tests/test_frameworks_have_integration_section.py` (plan 01-05) enforces this as an integration backstop.

### Forge Fields Produced

```json
{
  "ajtbd": {
    "job_id": "<int 1..8>",
    "segment_id": "<int 1..12>",
    "cited_audience_M": "<float — audience in millions>",
    "country_codes": ["<ISO-2>", "<ISO-2>", "<ISO-2>"],
    "evidence_urls": ["<URL-1>", "<URL-2>"],
    "trend_direction": "rising | stable | declining"
  }
}
```

Constraints:
- `job_id ∈ 1..8` — must match one of the 8 universal cinema JTBDs above
- `segment_id ∈ 1..12` — must match one of the 12 macro-segments above
- `country_codes` must contain ≥3 ISO-2 codes with evidence
- `evidence_urls` must contain ≥2 distinct domain URLs per audience claim
- `trend_direction` drawn from controlled vocabulary; "declining" reduces score

### Critic Re-Checks

- Trend evidence freshness: publication date of trend source must be ≤24 months from run date
- Audience claim must be ≥50M in absolute count (not percentage)
- ≥3 distinct countries with explicit mention in cited sources
- ≥2 distinct evidence-URL domains per claim (same domain cited twice = 1 source, not 2)
- `ajtbd_score` field in output is `None` until `pipeline/scoring.py::ajtbd_score` runs — LLM MUST NOT populate it (ADR-0002)

```
pipeline/scoring.py::ajtbd_score(
    cited_audience: int,

    trend_direction: str,

) -> int
```
