# Narrative Master Grid

## Frame

This file is the single canonical doctrine source for the Anomaly Engine Forge LLM. It contains five narrative grids — Polti 36, Booker 7, Tobias 20, Save the Cat 10, and Truby 4 — that the Forge uses to tag every generated concept with a deterministic narrative fingerprint. The combinatorial space across all five grids yields over 1.2 million situation-plot-genre combinations before assets are factored in; with audience assets and setting variables the figure rises to 1.24 × 10¹². This multiplier is the engine's core IP: it forces unlikely but structurally coherent pairings that LLM-only free association systematically misses. See PROJECT.md "Combinatorial multiplier" for the commercial rationale.

## The Five Grids

12. Obtaining

15. Murderous Adultery
16. Madness

19. Slaying of a Kinsman Unrecognized

### Booker — 7 Basic Plots

Christopher Booker's *The Seven Basic Plots* (2004) identifies seven universal narrative patterns drawn from cross-cultural story analysis. The Forge draws one Booker ID per concept.

Source: [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Seven_Basic_Plots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Seven_Basic_Plots)

1. **Overcoming the Monster** — protagonist faces an overwhelming antagonistic force threatening destruction (e.g., *Jaws*, *Alien*, *Get Out*)
2. **Rags to Riches** — protagonist rises from poverty or obscurity to wealth or recognition (e.g., *Slumdog Millionaire*, *Cinderella*)
3. **The Quest** — protagonist and companions travel to find an irreplaceable object or reach a distant place (e.g., *The Lord of the Rings*, *Mad Max: Fury Road*)
4. **Voyage and Return** — protagonist journeys to a strange world and returns transformed (e.g., *Alice in Wonderland*, *Spirited Away*, *The Wizard of Oz*)
5. **Comedy** — light-hearted conflicts arise from misunderstandings; resolution restores social harmony (e.g., *Four Weddings and a Funeral*)
6. **Tragedy** — protagonist's fatal flaw leads to downfall (e.g., *Macbeth*, *Requiem for a Dream*)
7. **Rebirth** — protagonist is trapped in a dark worldview until an event liberates them (e.g., *A Christmas Carol*, *Beauty and the Beast*, *Groundhog Day*)

**Worked example:** *The Martian* (Ridley Scott, 2015) — Booker 1 (Overcoming the Monster — the hostile planet) × Booker 3 (Quest — the rescue trajectory). [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martian_(film)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martian_(film))

5. Escape
6. Revenge
7. The Riddle
8. Rivalry
9. Underdog
10. Temptation
11. Metamorphosis
12. Transformation
13. Maturation
14. Love
15. Forbidden Love
16. Sacrifice
17. Discovery
18. Wretched Excess

20. Descension

**Worked example:** *Lady Bird* (Greta Gerwig, 2017) — Tobias 13 (Maturation) × 12 (Transformation). [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Bird_(film)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Bird_(film))

### Save the Cat — 10 Genres

Blake Snyder's *Save the Cat!* (2005) codified ten story genres that classify Hollywood films by structural function.

Source: [savethecat.com](https://savethecat.com) | [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Save_the_Cat!](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Save_the_Cat!)

1. **Monster in the House** — a monster (supernatural or metaphorical) threatens a confined group
2. **Golden Fleece** — road journey toward a prize; hero transformed by the trip
3. **Out of the Bottle** — wish granted, usually with comic/dark consequences
4. **Dude with a Problem** — ordinary person thrust into extraordinary life-or-death situation
5. **Rites of Passage** — life transitions: adolescence, divorce, middle age, death
6. **Buddy Love** — two opposites brought together; both transformed
7. **Whydunit** — societal evil exposed via detective-style investigation
8. **Fool Triumphant** — underestimated hero triumphs over a pompous establishment
9. **Institutionalized** — individual vs. oppressive group or institution
10. **Superhero** — extraordinary person in an ordinary world

**Worked example:** *Whiplash* (Damien Chazelle, 2014) — STC 9 (Institutionalized) × STC 5 (Rites of Passage). [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whiplash_(2014_film)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whiplash_(2014_film))

---

### Truby — 4 Grand-Genre Archetypes

John Truby's *The Anatomy of Story* (2007) identifies four grand-genre archetypes that underpin all specific genres.

Source: [truby.com](https://truby.com) | [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Truby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Truby)

1. **Myth** — hero embarks on a journey, faces trials, returns transformed; universal-collective resonance
2. **Memoir** — autobiographical arc; subjective interiority and personal revelation
3. **Wisdom** — protagonist learns life lessons; mentorship and growth
4. **Hero** — social champion; externalizes conflict into world-changing action

**Worked example:** *Black Panther* (Ryan Coogler, 2018) — Truby 1 (Myth) × Truby 4 (Hero). [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Panther_(film)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Panther_(film))

## Role Tuples

Every Forge concept declares six functional roles. Role tuples are separate from the five situation/plot grids and operate as a multiplier layer (6! permutations across character slots). Based on John Truby (*The Anatomy of Story*) and Christopher Vogler (*The Writer's Journey*, 1992).

Source: [truby.com](https://truby.com) | [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Writer%27s_Journey:_Mythic_Structure_for_Writers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Writer%27s_Journey:_Mythic_Structure_for_Writers)

| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| **Protagonist** | Primary agent of want; drives the story forward; Polti "Element A" |
| **Antagonist** | Active opposer of the protagonist's desire; "Element B" |
| **Ally** | Supports protagonist unconditionally; amplifies capacity |
| **Mentor** | Transfers knowledge or power; often sacrificed or withdrawn at Act 2 |
| **Catalyst** | Third-party force that triggers change without sustained involvement |
| **Mirror** | Reflects protagonist's arc; shows path-not-taken or inversion |

Role tuples are emitted per concept as `forge_meta.role_tuples[{role, character_id, archetype_source}]`.

## Coherence Anti-Pattern Matrix (preview)

The Forge enforces a structural coherence constraint: certain (polti_id, tobias_id) pairings are structurally incoherent — they share such overlapping dramatic DNA that the Forge cannot create genuine tension between them, only redundancy. The full constraint matrix lives at `pipeline/data/polti_tobias_coherence.json` (seeded in plan 01-04 with 5 bootstrap entries; audit alert triggers at >20 entries). At Forge time, the Forge must reject any concept where `(polti_id, tobias_id) ∈ anti_patterns`.

The file structure is: `{"anti_patterns": [[polti_id, tobias_id, "reason"], ...], "audit_threshold": 20}`.

## The Combinatorial Calculation

Five grids × role tuples yield the raw situation space:

**36 × 7 × 20 × 10 × 4 × 6 = 1,209,600**

(36 Polti situations × 7 Booker plots × 20 Tobias master plots × 10 STC genres × 4 Truby archetypes × 6 role permutation bases)

When audience assets, setting variables, and TRIZ contradiction indexes are layered in (see PROJECT.md "Combinatorial multiplier"), the effective space grows to **1.24 × 10¹²**.

> **This is a recitation, not arithmetic — verified by `pipeline/scoring.py` docstring in P3.** The LLM MUST NOT independently compute this figure (ADR-0002). The P3 function `pipeline.scoring.polti_tobias_coherence()` enforces the structural constraint check at runtime.

## How to Use This File at Forge Time

Rules for the Forge LLM when generating a concept:

1. **Draw one ID per grid:** draw `polti_id ∈ 1..36`, `booker_id ∈ 1..7`, `tobias_id ∈ 1..20`, `stc_id ∈ 1..10`, `truby_id ∈ 1..4` independently (with replacement across concepts, without replacement within a single batch run).
2. **Emit forge_meta:** emit `forge_meta.narrative_master_grid.{polti_id, booker_id, tobias_id, stc_id, truby_id, role_tuples}` per concept — these IDs are canonical identifiers downstream.
3. **Anti-pattern gate:** ensure `(polti_id, tobias_id) ∉ pipeline/data/polti_tobias_coherence.json#anti_patterns` before finalizing concept; if blocked, draw a new `tobias_id` and retry once.
4. **Role tuple assignment:** assign all six roles (`protagonist`, `antagonist`, `ally`, `mentor`, `catalyst`, `mirror`) to concrete character IDs; do not leave any role empty.
5. **Grid rationale:** include one sentence per grid explaining why this particular combination serves the concept's SDT primary need (from `frameworks/sdt-spine.md`).
6. **No improvised plots:** do not invent additional grids or numbered classifications outside these five; the Critic agent validates grid IDs against these exact lists.
7. **Coherence over novelty:** prefer pairings where the Polti situation and the Tobias master plot create dramatic tension rather than reinforce the same emotional note.

### Critic Re-Checks

- Verify `(polti_id, tobias_id) ∉ pipeline/data/polti_tobias_coherence.json#anti_patterns`
- Verify all six role_tuple slots are filled (no nulls)
- Verify `polti_id ∈ 1..36`, `booker_id ∈ 1..7`, `tobias_id ∈ 1..20`, `stc_id ∈ 1..10`, `truby_id ∈ 1..4`

### P3 Target Function

`pipeline/scoring.py::polti_tobias_coherence(polti_id: int, tobias_id: int) -> bool`

Returns `True` if the pairing is in the anti-pattern registry (Forge must redraw). Cites this file per ADR-0005.
