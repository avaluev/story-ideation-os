# Cinema-School Doctrines — The 10-School Floor

## Frame

The Anomaly Engine applies a **10-school doctrinal floor** as one of its
hardest mechanical filters. Every concept generated by the Forge must pass
at least 7 of 10 cinema-school checks. The rationale: film schools represent
distinct craft traditions with deeply different answers to the question "what
makes a film worth making?" A concept that satisfies only one or two of these
traditions is a narrow idea. A concept that satisfies 7 or more is — by
construction — simultaneously intelligible to commercial mainstream craft
(USC, NYU), European auteur tradition (FAMU, Lodz, VGIK), and Asian Fifth
Generation realism (Beijing). That is the originality corridor the engine
is designed to inhabit.

The 10-school floor is enforced as follows:

1. The Forge LLM emits `forge_meta.ten_school_self_check`: a mapping of
   school name to boolean (10 entries). The self-grade must be honest —
   not all True. A concept that claims to pass all 10 schools will be
   treated by the Critic as evidence of grade inflation and will trigger
   an automatic re-grade.

2. The Critic agent independently re-grades all 10 booleans. The concept
   passes the floor if and only if both the Forge self-check AND the Critic
   re-grade return >= 7 True values.

3. The cross-validation test `evals/test_school_checks.py` (written in P4)
   verifies that the self-check and re-grade agree on >= 8 of 10 entries.
   Disagreement on more than 2 entries triggers a human review flag.

The 10 schools and their doctrines are listed below. Each school entry
includes: a core doctrine, an LLM-applicable boolean check rule, an exemplar
alumnus with a representative film and citation URL, and the named boolean
check function used by `pipeline/scoring.py`.

## The 10 Schools

### USC (Cinematic Arts)

**Official site:** [cinema.usc.edu](https://cinema.usc.edu)

**Core doctrine:** USC's School of Cinematic Arts is the oldest and largest
film school in the United States. Its doctrine centers on **visual storytelling
with explicit three-act structure**: every film must have a discernible
opening image, midpoint visual reversal, and climax image whose relationship
is legible without dialogue. The school trains for the studio system — the
concept must be "green-lightable" in a commercial context, with a genre
container, a marketable premise, and a protagonist whose goal drives
continuous forward narrative momentum. Producer-director collaboration is
a structural value; the director is responsible for realizing a commercially
viable vision, not expressing an unmediated personal statement.

**Boolean check rule (`usc_check`):** The concept has explicit visual-driven
act breaks — an opening image that establishes the protagonist's world,
a midpoint visual reversal that shows the world changing irrevocably, and
a climax image that resolves or crystallizes the TRIZ contradiction. All
three are specifiable without dialogue. The concept fits within a recognized
commercial genre container (thriller, drama, sci-fi, etc.).

**Exemplar alumnus:** Ryan Coogler (USC MFA 2011). Representative film:
*Fruitvale Station* (2013). [imdb.com/title/tt2334649](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2334649)
George Lucas (USC 1966). *American Graffiti* (1973). [imdb.com/title/tt0069704](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0069704)

### UCLA (TFT)

**Official site:** [tft.ucla.edu](https://www.tft.ucla.edu)

**Core doctrine:** UCLA's School of Theater, Film and Television favors
**author-driven independent filmmaking and formal experimentation**. The
school's tradition is rooted in the L.A. Rebellion (the 1970s-1980s movement
of independent Black filmmakers trained there) and in a general preference
for the director as the film's primary author. Documentary, experimental, and
hybrid forms are valued alongside narrative. The concept must be legible as
a personal statement — the filmmaker's distinct perspective must be
identifiable in the subject matter, the formal choices, or both.

**Boolean check rule (`ucla_check`):** The concept's protagonist is clearly
the author's surrogate or the film makes one distinctive personal statement
that could not come from a neutral perspective. The concept has a formal
commitment — to a genre, to a documentary mode, to a hybrid structure —
that reflects an authorial choice rather than a market calculation.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Tim Burton (CalArts, with UCLA connections; representative
of the TFT experimental aesthetic). For a direct alumnus: Charles Burnett
(UCLA MFA). *Killer of Sheep* (1978). [imdb.com/title/tt0077237](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0077237)

---

### AFI (American Film Institute Conservatory)

**Official site:** [afi.com/conservatory](https://www.afi.com/conservatory)

**Core doctrine:** The AFI Conservatory trains director-as-auteur in a
**five-discipline fellowship model** (directing, producing, screenwriting,
cinematography, production design), with the director responsible for the
integrated vision across all disciplines. The doctrine is auteur-craft: the
director as the originating intelligence whose vision is realized through
complete technical and collaborative mastery. Small films made with full
artistic intention are valued over large films made by committee. Every AFI
Fellow directs a thesis film; the thesis is evaluated on craft excellence
rather than commercial potential.

**Boolean check rule (`afi_check`):** The concept has a single, identifiable
directorial vision that governs all major craft decisions — not just the
narrative, but the visual grammar, the sound design intention, and the
production design world. The concept is specifiable as a coherent artistic
intention that a single director-author could own completely.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Terrence Malick (AFI 1969). *Badlands* (1973).
[imdb.com/title/tt0069762](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0069762)
David Lynch (AFI 1966-68). *Eraserhead* (1977).
[imdb.com/title/tt0074486](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0074486)

---

### NYU Tisch (Grad Film)

**Official site:** [tisch.nyu.edu/film-tv](https://tisch.nyu.edu/film-tv)

**Core doctrine:** NYU Tisch Graduate Film favors **indie naturalism and
auteur ensemble filmmaking**: character-driven stories with documentary
aesthetic roots, often urban, often drawn from the filmmaker's lived experience
or community. The Cassavetes tradition is the canonical reference point —
improvisation within structure, emotional truth over plot mechanics, an
ensemble of characters with roughly equal moral weight. The concept must feel
like it could have been observed rather than invented.

**Boolean check rule (`nyu_check`):** The concept is rooted in a specific,
observed social reality (a community, a neighborhood, a profession, an
institution) that gives the fictional events the texture of documentary.
The protagonist's world is legible as a real place that real people inhabit.
The dramatic conflict emerges from character and social context rather than
from a plot mechanism external to the characters.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Martin Scorsese (NYU BFA 1966). *Mean Streets* (1973).
[imdb.com/title/tt0070379](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0070379)
Spike Lee (NYU MFA 1982). *She's Gotta Have It* (1986).
[imdb.com/title/tt0092088](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0092088)

---

### Columbia (School of the Arts)

**Official site:** [arts.columbia.edu/film](https://arts.columbia.edu/film)

**Core doctrine:** Columbia's film division prioritizes **screenwriting craft
and character-centered narrative**: the screenplay as a literary document
with full dramatic architecture. The Columbia tradition draws on the school's
proximity to the New York literary and theatrical world; the emphasis is on
dialogue, psychological depth, and moral complexity. Concepts are evaluated
on the quality of their premise (in Egri's sense) and the specificity of
their characters' inner lives.

**Boolean check rule (`columbia_check`):** The concept has a fully specified
premise (an Egri-style "A leads to B leads to C" moral argument) that is
distinct from a summary of the plot. The protagonist's inner life is specific
enough to support three full acts of psychological development without
requiring external plot machinery to sustain it.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Kasi Lemmons (Columbia-adjacent training; representative
of Columbia's character-first ethic). Canonical direct alumnus: James Mangold.
*Walk the Line* (2005). [imdb.com/title/tt0368794](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0368794)

---

### NFTS (UK National Film and Television School)

**Official site:** [nfts.co.uk](https://www.nfts.co.uk)

**Core doctrine:** The National Film and Television School trains filmmakers
in **integrated crew excellence across production disciplines**, with particular
strength in producing, editing, and documentary. The NFTS doctrine holds that
a film is a collaborative industrial object: the director's vision is necessary
but insufficient — the concept must be producible by a professional crew
working within practical constraints. Genre competence and producing viability
are evaluated alongside directorial vision.

**Boolean check rule (`nfts_check`):** The concept is producible within
a realistic budget range given its locations, cast requirements, and technical
demands. The genre is specified clearly enough that a professional producer
could estimate a production approach. The concept does not require
impossible practical effects or unpermittable locations to function.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Ridley Scott (NFTS founding committee member and
associated faculty). *The Duellists* (1977).
[imdb.com/title/tt0076248](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0076248)
Edgar Wright (attended NFTS). *Shaun of the Dead* (2004).
[imdb.com/title/tt0365748](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0365748)

---

### FAMU (Prague)

**Official site:** [famu.cz](https://www.famu.cz/en/)

**Core doctrine:** FAMU (Film and TV School of the Academy of Performing Arts
in Prague) is the home of **Czech poetic realism and the Eastern European
auteur tradition**. Its doctrine favors allegory, political subtext, and the
use of ordinary life as a frame for systemic social critique. The Czech New
Wave (Milos Forman, Jiri Menzel, Jan Nemec) was trained at FAMU; the tradition
privileges the specific detail of quotidian existence as the vehicle for
meaning — the absurdity of bureaucracy, the dignity of small resistance, the
comedy inside tragedy. The concept must have a layer of meaning beneath its
surface narrative.

**Boolean check rule (`famu_check`):** The concept has a second layer of
meaning (allegorical, political, or systemic) that is accessible without
being schematic. The surface narrative can be understood as a complete story
by a viewer who misses the second layer; the second layer rewards a more
attentive reading. The specific details of the concept's world are not
generic but are drawn from a particular social reality.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Milos Forman (FAMU 1957). *One Flew Over the Cuckoo's
Nest* (1975). [imdb.com/title/tt0073486](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0073486)
Jiri Menzel (FAMU 1962). *Closely Watched Trains* (1966).
[imdb.com/title/tt0060335](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0060335)

### Lodz (Poland)

**Official site:** [filmschool.lodz.pl](https://filmschool.lodz.pl)

**Core doctrine:** The Lodz Film School (Panstwowa Wyzsze Szkola Filmowa
Telewizyjna i Teatralna im. Leona Schillera; Lodz, Poland, founded 1948)
is the home of **political-historical cinema and visual essayism**. Its
graduates — Roman Polanski (Lodz 1953-1959), Andrzej Wajda (Lodz 1950-1953),
Krzysztof Kieslowski (Lodz 1964-1968) — define a tradition in which the
weight of recent history (World War II, the Holocaust, Soviet occupation,
Solidarity) is present in every story as both subject and formal constraint.
The Lodz doctrine values the moral seriousness of the historical record and
the filmmaker's responsibility to bear witness. Visual composition is treated
as an ethical as well as aesthetic choice.

**Boolean check rule (`lodz_check`):** The concept is morally serious: it
takes a position on the ethical weight of its events and does not treat its
subject matter as mere entertainment. The concept either engages with
historical or political reality directly, or treats its fictional world with
the gravity that historical reality demands. The visual and formal choices
implied by the concept are not arbitrary — they are ethically motivated by
what the story is about.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Roman Polanski (Lodz 1953-1959). *The Pianist* (2002).
[imdb.com/title/tt0253474](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0253474)
Andrzej Wajda (Lodz 1950-1953). *Ashes and Diamonds* (1958).
[imdb.com/title/tt0050700](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0050700)
Krzysztof Kieslowski (Lodz 1964-1968). *Three Colors: Blue* (1993).
[imdb.com/title/tt0108394](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0108394)

### VGIK (Moscow)

**Official site:** [vgik.info](https://www.vgik.info)

**Core doctrine:** VGIK (the All-Russian State University of Cinematography,
founded 1919 by Lev Kuleshov) is the oldest film school in the world and
the institutional home of **Eisenstein montage and the Soviet montage
tradition**. The doctrine holds that meaning is created not within the shot
but in the collision between shots: the edit is the primary creative act. The
Kuleshov Effect, Eisenstein's intellectual montage, and Vertov's kino-eye
are the canonical references. At VGIK, the concept must contain at least one
sequence whose meaning is created by edited contrast rather than by the content
of any single shot.

**Boolean check rule (`vgik_check`):** The concept includes at least one
montage-of-juxtaposition sequence where edited contrast is the meaning-bearer
— two or more images whose collision produces an idea that neither image
contains individually. The concept's structure has at least one moment where
the meaning is in the transition, not in the content.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Andrei Tarkovsky (VGIK 1956-1960, workshop of Mikhail
Romm). *Andrei Rublev* (1966).
[imdb.com/title/tt0060107](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0060107)
Alexander Sokurov (VGIK 1974-1979). *Russian Ark* (2002).
[imdb.com/title/tt0318840](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0318840)

---

### Beijing Film Academy

**Official site:** [bfa.edu.cn](http://www.bfa.edu.cn)

**Core doctrine:** The Beijing Film Academy (founded 1950) trained the **Fifth
Generation of Chinese filmmakers** — the graduating class of 1982 who had
survived the Cultural Revolution and who produced a cinema of **visual
lyricism and historical witness**. The Fifth Generation doctrine: the landscape
is a character; color is a meaning-system; the individual story is always
also the national story; and the camera must find the angle that reveals the
historical weight of ordinary moments. Zhang Yimou's use of red in *Ju Dou*,
Chen Kaige's use of space in *Yellow Earth* — these are not stylistic choices
but ethical commitments about how to render a historical reality.

**Boolean check rule (`beijing_check`):** The concept's visual world is
fully specified enough to function as a meaning-system independent of the
dialogue. The landscape, color palette, and spatial relationships of the
concept's world are not neutral but encode the concept's thematic argument.
At least one visual element is doing double duty — simultaneously depicting
a narrative reality and embodying the concept's central concern.

**Exemplar alumnus:** Zhang Yimou (Beijing Film Academy 1978-1982).
*Raise the Red Lantern* (1991).
[imdb.com/title/tt0101640](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0101640)
Chen Kaige (Beijing Film Academy 1978-1982). *Farewell My Concubine* (1993).
[imdb.com/title/tt0106332](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0106332)

## How a Concept Passes 7/10

The Forge emits the 10-bool map in `forge_meta.ten_school_self_check` using
the exact school names listed below:

```
ten_school_self_check = {
    "USC":      true | false,
    "UCLA":     true | false,
    "AFI":      true | false,
    "NYU":      true | false,
    "Columbia": true | false,
    "NFTS":     true | false,
    "FAMU":     true | false,
    "Lodz":     true | false,
    "VGIK":     true | false,
    "Beijing":  true | false
}
```

**Honest self-grade.** A concept that returns all 10 as True is lying. The
10 schools represent genuinely different and sometimes mutually exclusive
commitments: a concept optimized for USC's commercial three-act structure
may genuinely fail VGIK's montage-of-juxtaposition test; a concept optimized
for FAMU's allegorical subtlety may genuinely fail NYU's observed-social-
reality test. The Forge must apply each check rule honestly and mark False
where the concept does not satisfy the criterion.

**Critic re-grade.** The Critic agent independently applies all 10 check
rules to the concept text. If the Critic's re-grade disagrees with the Forge
self-check on more than 2 of the 10 entries, the concept is flagged for
human review before scoring proceeds.

**Threshold.** Pass = (Forge True count >= 7) AND (Critic True count >= 7).
A concept that scores 7 on self-check but 6 on Critic re-grade fails the
floor. Both counts must reach the threshold independently.

## Operational Integration

This section is the canonical interface between `cinema-school-doctrines.md`
and the Anomaly Engine pipeline. All fields listed here are produced by the
Forge and validated by the Critic and the P3 scoring layer.

### Forge Fields Produced

| Field | Type | Values | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ten_school_self_check` | dict | 10 school name -> bool | All 10 school sections above |
| `ten_school_self_check.USC` | bool | true/false | `usc_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.UCLA` | bool | true/false | `ucla_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.AFI` | bool | true/false | `afi_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.NYU` | bool | true/false | `nyu_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.Columbia` | bool | true/false | `columbia_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.NFTS` | bool | true/false | `nfts_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.FAMU` | bool | true/false | `famu_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.Lodz` | bool | true/false | `lodz_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.VGIK` | bool | true/false | `vgik_check` rule |
| `ten_school_self_check.Beijing` | bool | true/false | `beijing_check` rule |

### Critic Re-checks

The Critic independently applies the 10 boolean check rules defined in each
school section above. The Critic's output is stored in
`critic_meta.ten_school_regrade` (same schema as `ten_school_self_check`).
The cross-validation test `evals/test_school_checks.py` (created in P4)
verifies that:

1. Both dicts have exactly 10 keys matching the school names above.
2. `sum(ten_school_self_check.values()) >= 7` (Forge floor met).
3. `sum(ten_school_regrade.values()) >= 7` (Critic floor met).
4. Agreement count `>= 8` (Forge and Critic agree on at least 8 of 10).

### P3 Scoring Target

```
pipeline/scoring.py::school_pass_count(ten_school_self_check: dict) -> int
```

Returns the integer count of True values in the `ten_school_self_check` dict
(range 0..10). The threshold check `>= 7` is applied after the function
returns. The scoring function iterates over the 10 fixed school-name keys
listed above; any missing key is treated as False. The function does not
import from `frameworks/` (ADR-0005) — the school name constants are defined
directly in `pipeline/scoring.py` with a citation comment pointing to this
section.
