### IF Booker Plot = Overcoming the Monster (6 beats)

**Setup (0–N min):** [Ordinary world. Protagonist in their element. Hint of the monster/threat.]
**Anticipation (N–N min):** [Hero becomes aware. First failed attempt to understand or neutralize threat.]
**Frustration (N–N min):** [Monster grows dominant. Hero's methods fail. Cost begins to compound.]
**Nightmare (N–N min):** [Protagonist at lowest point. Monster fully dominant. All seems lost. The ghost surfaces.]
**Miraculous Release (N–N min):** [The shift. The weapon or insight found. Decisive confrontation initiated.]
**Triumph (N–N min):** [Monster defeated. Resolution. What has changed in the world.]

---
### IF Booker Plot = Rags to Riches (5 beats)

**Initial World (0–N min):** [Protagonist's constraint. The gap between where they are and where they must go.]
**The Call (N–N min):** [The opportunity. First step toward the new world. Flaw seeds planted.]
**Initial Success + Shadow (N–N min):** [Progress. Cost not yet visible. Fatal flaw begins to operate.]
**Central Crisis (N–N min):** [Flaw crystallizes. Everything gained is at risk. Forced choice.]
**Independence and Completion (N–N min):** [True earning of the new position. Cost paid. Resolution.]

---
### IF Booker Plot = The Quest (5 beats)

**Initial Predicament (0–N min):** [World broken. Quest object identified. Protagonist called or pushed.]
**Journey Begins (N–N min):** [Companions assembled. First obstacles. Rules of the road established.]
**Journey + Ordeals (N–N min):** [Series of trials. Companions tested. Shadow of final obstacle grows.]
**Final Ordeal (N–N min):** [Approach to the goal. Highest stakes. The real nature of the quest revealed.]
**Goal Achieved (N–N min):** [What is found is not what was sought. Resolution. World changed or not.]

---
### IF Booker Plot = Voyage and Return (6 beats)

**Anticipation (0–N min):** [Ordinary world. The pull or push toward the other world.]
**Fall (N–N min):** [Entry into the other world. Initial fascination. Wonder.]
**Frustration (N–N min):** [The other world turns hostile. Protagonist trapped or exposed. Darkness creeps.]
**Nightmare (N–N min):** [Full threat revealed. Maximum danger. No way out visible.]
**Escape (N–N min):** [The turn. The escape mechanism. Cost of exit.]
**Return (N–N min):** [Back in the ordinary world. What is brought back. What has changed.]

---
### IF Booker Plot = Comedy (5 beats)

**World of Shadows (0–N min):** [Characters in wrong relationships. Deception or misunderstanding established.]
**Tightening (N–N min):** [Confusion compounds. Characters pulled further from what they need.]
**Knot (N–N min):** [Maximum confusion. No resolution visible. All relationships mis-aligned.]
**Unknotting (N–N min):** [Truth begins to emerge. Dominos fall in sequence.]
**Resolution (N–N min):** [Correct relationships established. Genuine connection. World made whole.]

---
### IF Booker Plot = Tragedy (6 beats — Freytag)

**Exposition (0–N min):** [World and protagonist's stature. Fatal flaw introduced implicitly.]
**Rising Action (N–N min):** [Ambition rises. Early success. Flaw seeds planted in choices made.]
**Climax / Peripeteia (N–N min):** [Reversal. Flaw crystallizes. Point of no return crossed.]
**Falling Action (N–N min):** [Consequences compound. Protagonist doubles down. Loss accelerates.]
**Catastrophe + Anagnorisis (N–N min):** [Recognition. Destruction. What remains. The argument of the film stated by outcome.]

---
### IF Booker Plot = Rebirth (5 beats)

**Under the Shadow (0–N min):** [Protagonist under dark force or limiting belief. Partial life.]
**Threat Emerges (N–N min):** [External event forces reckoning. Hero's world contracts.]
**Long Shadow (N–N min):** [Dark force dominant. Protagonist adapts by shrinking. Nearly lost.]
**Crisis Point (N–N min):** [The moment the hero must act or be destroyed. Final offer of transformation.]
**Release (N–N min):** [Liberation from the shadow. Full life possible. Cost of the journey honored.]
