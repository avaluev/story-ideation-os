# Intellectual Framework — 7-Domain Evaluation Protocol
## Used by: concept-challenger agent (Phase 2 Domain Analysis)
## Select 3–5 most relevant criteria per concept

---

## Domain 1: Philosophy of Aesthetics
### Source: Kant, Hume, Goodman, Danto, Dickie (institutional theory)

**AES-01 (Kant) — Aesthetic Disinterestedness**
Does the film generate an experience that cannot be fully reduced to its plot utility?
PASS: concept produces aesthetic surplus — something felt but not fully explained
FAIL: concept is pure genre delivery; remove the plot and nothing remains

**AES-02 (Hume) — Intersubjective Resonance**
Would trained aesthetic observers across cultures converge on the same quality assessment?
PASS: concept would produce convergent assessment across cultural contexts
FAIL: appreciation requires cultural insider knowledge to access

**AES-03 (Goodman) — Fit for Purpose**
Is the chosen format (feature / series / animation) the only right vehicle for this premise?
PASS: the premise cannot be told as effectively in any other format
FAIL: premise would work equally well — or better — in a different format

**AES-04 (Danto) — Artworld Framing**
Does it work as entertainment AND as aesthetic artifact?
PASS: functions in commercial distribution AND generates critical/aesthetic discourse
FAIL: requires festival context or art-house framing to find its audience

**AES-05 (Dickie) — Institutional Theory**
Will cultural gatekeepers (critics, programmers, distributors) recognize it as a valid discourse entry?
PASS: concept generates critical response and aesthetic claims, not only consumer response
FAIL: concept is a market product only; no aesthetic claim survives outside commercial context

## Domain 2: Creativity Theory
### Source: Boden's taxonomy, Csikszentmihalyi's systems model, Simonton's historiometrics

**CRE-01 (Boden) — Creativity Type**
Which of three types does this concept exemplify?
- Combinatorial: new combination of existing ideas (lowest novelty ceiling)
- Exploratory: explores edges of an established conceptual space (medium ceiling)
- Transformational: restructures the conceptual space itself (highest ceiling; rarest)
Required: state which type and provide evidence from the concept's structure.
PASS if transformational or exploratory with clear boundary-pushing evidence.
FLAG if combinatorial — state what makes it valuable despite the lower ceiling.

**CRE-02 (Csikszentmihalyi) — Systems Model (Person × Domain × Field)**
Person: Does the concept reflect genuine individual creative perspective beyond training-data patterns?
Domain: Does it engage a domain with established rules it meaningfully bends?
Field: Will gatekeepers (critics, programmers, distributors) recognize and reward its value?
PASS: all three dimensions active.
CONDITIONAL: two of three active; note the gap.
FAIL: only one dimension active.

**CRE-03 (Simonton) — Chance-Configuration**
Is the insight chance-permutated (random collision selected by fitness) or deliberately engineered?
Required: state which. If chance-configured, assess the quality of the selective retention mechanism.
PASS: strong selective retention — the collision was filtered by genuine criteria.
FAIL: collision is random; no selection criterion other than "interesting."

**CRE-04 (Csikszentmihalyi) — Flow Potential**
Will the audience reach flow state — full absorption where time disappears?
Required: identify the flow-induction mechanism (escalating stakes, character investment, puzzle structure).
PASS: mechanism identifiable in concept structure.
FAIL: no mechanism; concept generates curiosity but not absorption.

**CRE-05 — Retrospective Fallacy Assessment**
Will this be recognized as great immediately, or only in retrospect?
Evidence cases: Blade Runner (1982, critical failure → masterpiece), Children of Men (2006, modest theatrical → canonical), Parasite (2019, immediate consensus).
Required: which trajectory? Cite one analogous film.
This is not a kill condition — it is a distribution and positioning signal.

## Domain 3: Cognitive Science of Creativity
### Source: Andrews-Hanna (DMN), Wallas 4-stage, Mednick (remote associates), Guilford (divergent thinking), Kounios & Beeman (insight)

**COG-01 (DMN) — Default Mode Network Engagement**
Great narratives activate the DMN during reflection moments — audiences daydream productively.
Required: identify the incubation space in the concept — where does the audience's mind wander between scenes?
PASS: explicit incubation space (unresolved question, lingering image, withheld information).
FAIL: concept is wall-to-wall information delivery with no room for mental wandering.

**COG-02 (Guilford) — Dual-Process Balance**
Divergent thinking (generating possibilities) must precede convergent thinking (selecting the answer).
Required: identify where the concept forces divergent thinking in the audience.
PASS: audience generates multiple interpretations or predictions before convergence.
FAIL: concept tells the audience what to think at every beat; no divergent space.

**COG-03 (Mednick) — Remote Associates**
Creative breakthroughs combine distant elements via a non-obvious associative bridge.
Required: name the two most remote conceptual elements in the premise. Estimate cosine similarity (0.30–0.50 = optimal).
State the named bridge that makes the distant elements cohere.
PASS: bridge is non-obvious but once seen, inevitable.
FAIL: bridge is absent (elements remain separate) or trivial (elements were never distant).

**COG-04 (Wallas) — 4-Stage Creative Model**
Map the concept's structure to Wallas's stages:
- Preparation (exposition: audience learns the rules of the world)
- Incubation (rising action: the problem works on the audience below the threshold of attention)
- Illumination (the "Aha!" moment — the MDL Delta event)
- Verification (resolution: the illumination is tested and confirmed)
Required: identify which scene or beat corresponds to each stage.
PASS: all 4 stages identifiable in the synopsis.
CONDITIONAL: 3 of 4 stages identifiable; note the gap.

**COG-05 — Filmmaker Process Reference**
Required: cite ≥1 documented creative process from a named filmmaker whose approach resembles this concept's generation.
Use from: Kubrick's index-card systems, Tarkovsky's Sculpting in Time journal, Lynch's "Catching the Big Fish" intuition method, Miyazaki's non-outline process, Bong Joon-ho's storyboard-first methodology, Nolan's scene-puzzle construction, Kaufman's structural deconstruction, Sorkin's walk-and-talk scene composition.
Explain: what specific element of this concept resembles that filmmaker's documented method?

## Domain 4: Narrative Structure
### Source: Aristotle, Campbell/Vogler, Snyder, Reagan/Kim/Dodds 2016, McKee, Egri

**STR-01 (Aristotle) — Classical Structure**
Does each of Beginning / Middle / End have a distinct causal function?
PASS: clear causality between sections; Middle could not be removed without destroying the End.
FAIL: Middle is a series of disconnected events that don't causally generate the End.

**STR-02 (Campbell/Vogler) — Monomyth Mapping**
Map the synopsis to Vogler's 12 stages. Note which are present and which are deliberately absent.
12 stages: Ordinary World, Call to Adventure, Refusal of the Call, Meeting the Mentor, Crossing the Threshold, Tests/Allies/Enemies, Approach to Inmost Cave, Ordeal, Reward, Road Back, Resurrection, Return with Elixir.
Required: identify present stages + explain intentional omissions.

**STR-03 (Snyder) — Save the Cat Alignment**
Identify which of Snyder's 15 beats appear. Does the opening image mirror the final image with transformation?
15 beats: Opening Image, Theme Stated, Set-Up, Catalyst, Debate, Break into Two, B Story, Fun and Games, Midpoint, Bad Guys Close In, All Is Lost, Dark Night of the Soul, Break into Three, Finale, Final Image.
Required: note which beats are present + whether opening/final images mirror with transformation.

**STR-04 (Reagan 2016) — Computational Arc Confirmation**
Confirm the Reagan arc shape stated in Framework Tags.
Evidence: which synopsis beats produce emotional rise/fall?
Required: map 2–3 specific beats to the stated arc shape.
Revenue premium: state the Del Vecchio 2020 domestic average for this arc shape.

**STR-05 — McKee 3-Level Conflict**
Inner conflict: [protagonist's internal contradiction]
Personal conflict: [protagonist vs. specific other character]
Extra-personal conflict: [protagonist vs. institution/society/nature]
All 3 must operate simultaneously by Act II midpoint.
PASS: all 3 levels stated and operating.
FAIL: only 1 or 2 levels active.

**STR-06 (Egri) — Theme and Controlling Idea**
State the controlling idea exactly: "[Positive value] through [cause] leads to [consequence]."
Does every major scene in the synopsis advance or test this premise?
PASS: premise survives as a claim after plot details are removed.
FAIL: premise is a mood, not an argument.

**STR-07 — Genre-Bending Assessment**
When does genre-bending win (Star Wars, Get Out, Parasite) vs. lose (The Last Jedi audience reaction)?
Required: is this concept bending genre in a way that satisfies the 70/30 rule (70% familiar, 30% subverted)?
State what the 70% is and what the 30% is.

## Domain 5: Market Science
### Source: EPAGOGIX, Parrot Analytics, Berger STEPPS, Dawkins/Blackmore (memetics), Katzenberg "high concept" history

**MKT-01 — High-Concept Test (Katzenberg/Spielberg era)**
Can the concept be stated in ≤25 words for a greenlight committee?
Required: write the exact ≤25-word statement.
PASS: speakable in one breath, no subordinate clauses, no domain knowledge required.
FAIL: requires explanation to be exciting.

**MKT-02 (Berger) — STEPPS Score**
Score each dimension Y/N:
- Social Currency: sharing this makes people look smart or interesting [Y/N + rationale]
- Triggers: recurring cue that brings the premise to mind unprompted [Y/N + what triggers it]
- Emotion: produces a strong emotional response on premise exposure alone [Y/N + emotion named]
- Public: the behavior or theme is publicly visible, not hidden [Y/N]
- Practical Value: provides useful knowledge or insight [Y/N]
- Stories: inherently narrative — people naturally retell it [Y/N]
Score: [N/6]. PASS if ≥4. Report each dimension.

**MKT-03 — Travelability Assessment**
Is the concept domestic-only / regional / global?
Required: name specific territories + one rationale sentence per territory.
Reference cases: Korean wave (Parasite), Bollywood (domestic + diaspora), Japanese anime (niche global), French auteur (festival circuit global).

**MKT-04 — Star Power vs. Concept Strength**
Can this concept be marketed without star attachment?
PASS: logline generates interest independent of cast.
FAIL: concept requires a star to make the pitch legible.
Required: cite one comparable concept-first marketing campaign (e.g., Get Out, A Quiet Place, Hereditary).

**MKT-05 (Snyder) — Logline Sellability**
Does the logline communicate: irony, compelling mental image, audience and cost?
Snyder's 4 elements: (1) an ironic premise, (2) a compelling mental image, (3) a sense of audience, (4) a sense of cost/budget.
Required: assess each of the 4 elements.

**MKT-06 — Zeitgeist Alignment**
Why is 2026–2027 the specific right window?
Reference: Squid Game (economic anxiety + platform moment), Joker (disillusionment), Barbie (brand nostalgia + feminist discourse), Oppenheimer (historical anxiety + prestige hunger).
Required: cite the specific cultural conditions (named statistic or event) that make this moment right.
State: what would this concept be missing if made in 2020? In 2030?

**MKT-07 — Word-of-Mouth Contagion**
Can this concept be described in 10 seconds after viewing?
Required: write the exact 10-second description (≤25 words).
PASS: the 10-second version preserves the core emotional payload.
FAIL: requires 2 minutes to explain what made it good.

**MKT-08 — International Demand Variance**
Which distribution pattern applies?
Required: name the precedent and explain the parallel.

## Domain 6: Computational Creativity Scoring
### Source: Wiggins (creative systems), Ritchie (empirical criteria), Boden, MAP-Elites

**CC-01 (Ritchie) — Novelty**
Is the artifact genuinely new, or a recombination of existing tokens?
Required: identify what specific element has not appeared in prior work.

**CC-02 (Ritchie) — Quality**
Would practitioners in the domain (screenwriters, development executives, critics) recognize it as good?
Required: what professional criterion does it satisfy that similar concepts have failed to meet?

**CC-03 (Ritchie) — Typicality**
Is it recognizable as belonging to its genre while being novel? (Extremes fail: too typical = boring, too atypical = alienating)
Required: what makes it recognizable as [genre]? What makes it not a genre clone?

**CC-04 (Wiggins) — Creative System Dimension**
Which dimension dominates: Novelty (new to the world), Value (recognized as good), Surprise (unexpected given priors)?
Required: state which dimension is strongest and why.

**CC-05 (MAP-Elites) — Diversity Archive Position**
Where does this concept sit in novelty × resonance × viability space?
Novelty: [low / medium / high]
Resonance: [low / medium / high]
Viability: [low / medium / high]
This is the Pareto frontier assessment — great concepts maximize all three.

**CC-06 — AI Slop Defense**
What specific element prevents this from being indistinguishable from standard AI generation?
Required: name the one structural or thematic element that an LLM would not generate from pattern-matching alone.

## Domain 7: Failure Mode Taxonomy
### Source: Klein (premortem), documented development hell, AI slop pattern analysis

Apply all 8 failure modes. For each: state CLEAR / RISK / FAIL.

**FAIL-01 — Peters' Mandate**
Is there an arbitrary external constraint baked into the premise that the story cannot remove?
Examples: a character must behave stupidly for the plot to work; a coincidence must occur; the antagonist must be incompetent.
CLEAR: no arbitrary constraints. RISK: one weak constraint. FAIL: plot depends on it.

**FAIL-02 — High-Concept Vacuum**
Does the hook sustain 90 minutes (or the full series run) of character-driven conflict?
Test: remove the premise hook. Do the characters still have a story?
CLEAR: characters have independent dramatic needs. FAIL: without the hook, there is no story.

**FAIL-03 — Tonal Whiplash**
Are there un-signaled genre shifts baked into the concept?
Test: does the premise logically imply a tone? Does the synopsis violate it without preparation?
CLEAR: tone is consistent or signaled shifts are earned. FAIL: genre shifts are unannounced.

**FAIL-04 — Protagonist Agency Failure**
Can the protagonist drive the plot using their own choices?
Test: in each act, is the protagonist reacting to events or initiating them?
CLEAR: protagonist initiates ≥2 major plot developments. FAIL: protagonist is a passenger.

**FAIL-05 — Homework Coefficient Failure**
Does understanding the emotional stakes require >3 units of external knowledge?
Test: count external IP, lore systems, or world-building dependencies.
CLEAR: ≤2 dependencies. RISK: 3 dependencies. FAIL: ≥4 dependencies.

**FAIL-06 — Stale Zeitgeist**
Is the premise timely now, or was it timely 3–5 years ago?
Test: name a film that explored this cultural moment in 2019–2021. What does this concept add that was not already said?
CLEAR: adds new dimension. RISK: timely but well-covered territory. FAIL: the moment has passed.

**FAIL-07 — False Universal**
Does the concept assume a shared experience that is actually local or demographic-specific?
Test: does the premise require the audience to have had a specific life experience to feel the stakes?
CLEAR: stakes are universally accessible. RISK: stakes are culturally specific but bridged. FAIL: stakes are local with no bridge.

**FAIL-08 — AI Slop Signature**
Is the concept indistinguishable from common LLM generation patterns?
Check against these patterns:
- "An unlikely [X] must [Y] to save [Z]" with no structural subversion
- The protagonist's special ability is exactly what is needed to solve the problem
- The villain's motivation is power or control without personal dimension
- The resolution involves the protagonist "believing in themselves"
- The emotional climax is a speech or confession
CLEAR: none of the above patterns present without inversion. FAIL: ≥2 patterns present without inversion.
