# Showcase — the flagship 20-concept slate

This directory is proof-of-capability: a complete slate of **20 original film/series
concepts generated by the engine**, in English and Russian, with comparable-title
economics that were verified claim-by-claim against primary sources.

## What's here

| Path | Contents |

| [`concepts/EN/`](concepts/EN) | 20 English concept cards (`01_husbandry` … `20_wellkeeper`) |

| [`SLATE_SUMMARY.md`](SLATE_SUMMARY.md) | Slate-wide evidence metrics and per-concept grades |
| [`BEST_IDEAS_VERIFIED.md`](BEST_IDEAS_VERIFIED.md) | The headline picks |

## How the slate was verified

Every externally checkable claim in every card was handed to an independent
finder (which had to return a primary deep-link plus a ≤25-word verbatim
value-on-page quote), then to an independent refute-by-default verifier. Only
claims that survived both were marked verified. **Dollar figures were frozen
byte-for-byte throughout; nothing was fabricated.**

Measured result across the slate (see [`SLATE_SUMMARY.md`](SLATE_SUMMARY.md)):

- **167 of 200** external claims independently verified
- **88.8%** mean deep-link coverage · **89.2%** mean quote-bound coverage
- **0** fabricated claims
- Grade distribution: **10 A · 9 B · 1 C**

## What each concept card contains

- **Logline + tagline** — the hook in two lines.
- **Market & audience** — TAM → SAM → SOM with an explicit, auditable methodology block.
- **Year-1 SOM** — a `python_executed`, comparable-anchored figure with an 80% confidence band (never a number written by a language model).
- **Comparable titles** — real films with worldwide gross and ROI, each deep-linked to Box Office Mojo / The Numbers.
- **Revenue thesis, risk register, and the "why now."**
