# Amplified Slate — EN Evidence Run (Run B) Result

The twenty flagship concept reports were live-sourced claim-by-claim: every
externally checkable claim was handed to an independent finder agent (primary
deep-link + ≤25-word verbatim value-on-page quote), then to an independent
refute-by-default verifier. Verified judgments were subject-bound and merged
into each card's credibility score. Dollar figures were frozen byte-for-byte
throughout; nothing was fabricated.

## Honest limitations (see `_below_gate.json`)

Two strict definition-of-done floors are not universally met, for reasons that
are structural rather than sourcing failures:

1. **Twelve-deep-link floor (17 of 20 cards short).** Most cards contain only
   8–11 externally checkable claims, so even at full sourcing they cap at
   9–11 links. Reaching twelve would require adding comparables or claims to
   the card body — content padding the run deliberately did not do. Three cards
   that already carry enough claims (03, 07, 13) meet the floor.

2. **Six cards below 0.80 density.** The unverified remainder is dominated by
   (a) the engine's own python-computed projections that leaked into prose as
   "demand" claims — year-one obtainable capture, plausible ranges, lifetime
   tails — which are derived figures, not external facts, and are correctly left
   unverified rather than sourced to a borrowed URL; and (b) comparable
   return-multiples whose worldwide gross is verified on a single-film page but
   whose ROI multiple the page does not state — the multiple is a computed
   derivative, so those claims are held supported-not-verified rather than
   stretched to fit. A handful of editorial superlatives ("most durable
   category") are likewise left unverified by design.

No claim was lowered to a weaker source to clear a floor, and no number was
invented to raise a count.

## Method notes

- **Single-film URL binding.** A comparable's value on a Box Office Mojo
  `/title/` or `/release/` page (or a The Numbers `/movie/` page) is bound to the
  film by the URL itself — off-scope mismatch is structurally impossible on a
  one-film page — so a bare "Worldwide $X" quote is accepted there, while
  multi-title pages (year charts, lists) still require the title co-located with
  the value. This recovered legitimately-sourced comparables an earlier
  quote-only rule had dropped.
- **Adversarial verification.** Stragglers were re-verified by an Opus
  refute-by-default checker, which held a deliberately high bar on
  return-multiples and editorial claims; survivors are rock-solid.
- **Source mix.** Box Office Mojo / The Numbers title pages, MPA THEME
  ($328.2B), Ampere streaming ($157.1B), Parrot Analytics demand, plus Gallup /
  Pew / YouGov / KFF / Nielsen / Variety / Deadline primary reporting.
