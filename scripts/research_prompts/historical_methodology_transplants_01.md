From the history of science, legal history, archival studies, and epistemology, list 20 documented human methodologies for establishing truth, authenticity, or quality that were developed before 1950 and that face a genuine structural crisis in the 21st century due to technological change. Exclude any religiously specific examples — focus on secular, scientific, legal, or craft methodologies. For each: (1) name the era and domain of origin, (2) describe the methodology in plain English, (3) identify the specific 21st-century technological development that breaks one of the methodology's foundational assumptions, (4) describe the character who would embody this collision — trained in the old method, now confronting the new problem, (5) cite historical sources or academic papers documenting the methodology. Include examples from: forensic science, peer review, archival certification, legal chain of custody, diplomatic document authentication, numismatic and art authentication, astronomical observation recording, clinical trial methodology, investigative journalism, census-taking and demographic recording, cartographic verification, genealogical research.
