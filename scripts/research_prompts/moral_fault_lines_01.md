From moral philosophy, applied ethics, and cross-cultural moral psychology research, list 25 documented value conflicts that appear across cultures as genuine dilemmas — where both sides have a defensible moral position and neither side is simply wrong. For each: (1) describe the conflict in plain English, (2) cite the philosophical tradition or empirical research that documented it (Haidt moral foundations, Kohlberg stages, Gilligan care ethics, Rawls justice theory, virtue ethics tradition, cross-cultural moral psychology), (3) identify the population that most strongly holds each pole of the dilemma, (4) name one film or book that dramatized this specific conflict and its outcome. Prioritize conflicts that: cut across political lines, appear in all cultures, involve institutions vs individuals, involve competing goods rather than good vs evil. Sources: Haidt 'The Righteous Mind', Foot trolley problem research, MIT Moral Machine experiment (2.3M participants from 233 countries), World Values Survey data.
